Month-End Close Autopilot
Turn any trial balance into a complete narrative in 60 seconds
Month-end reporting Client communication ⏱ Saves 2–3 hrs/client/month
The prompt — copy & paste into ChatGPT or Claude
You are a senior accountant preparing a month-end narrative for a client. I will give you a trial balance with current month and prior month figures. Your job is to: 1. Identify the 5 most significant variances (by % and absolute value) 2. Write a plain-English explanation for each variance — what likely caused it 3. Flag any items that need investigation (unusual movements, sign changes, large one-offs) 4. Write a 3-paragraph executive summary suitable for a business owner 5. List 3 recommended actions the client should take this month Trial balance data: [PASTE YOUR TRIAL BALANCE HERE — copy from Xero, QuickBooks, or Sage] Business context: - Industry: [e.g. Retail / Services / Construction] - Business size: [e.g. 5 staff, $2M revenue] - Key concerns this month: [e.g. cash flow, new hire, slow sales] Format the output as: - Executive Summary (3 paragraphs) - Variance Analysis (table: Account | Prior | Current | Change % | Explanation) - Items for Investigation (bullet list) - Recommended Actions (numbered list)
Pro tips for best results
  • Copy your trial balance directly from Xero (Reports → Trial Balance → copy table)
  • Add context about the business — the more you give, the better the narrative
  • Use GPT-4o or Claude Sonnet 4 for best results on complex accounts
  • Edit the output in your own voice before sending to clients
  • Save your best outputs as templates for similar clients
  • Run it again mid-month as a forecast narrative using budget vs actual data
Example output (excerpt)
"Revenue grew 12% month-on-month, driven primarily by the Q3 pipeline converting in October. Gross margin held steady at 64%, though materials costs edged up 8% — worth monitoring heading into Q4. Net profit improved to $18,400, up from $11,200 last month — a 64% increase driven by the combination of top-line growth and disciplined cost control…"

Client Onboarding Script Generator
Generate a complete onboarding package from two inputs
New client onboarding Practice management ⏱ Saves 1–2 hrs/new client
The prompt — copy & paste into ChatGPT or Claude
You are an experienced bookkeeping practice manager onboarding a new client. Using only the two inputs below, generate a complete new-client onboarding package: Client industry: [e.g. Restaurant / E-commerce / Professional Services / Construction] Accounting software: [e.g. Xero / QuickBooks Online / Sage] Generate the following: 1. WELCOME EMAIL (professional, warm, 200 words) - Introduce yourself and the practice - Set expectations for communication and turnaround - List the 3 things you need from them in the first week 2. ONBOARDING CHECKLIST (20 items) - Software setup tasks (chart of accounts, bank feeds, users) - Information to collect from client (registration numbers, bank details, prior year data) - Industry-specific compliance items - First month deliverables 3. FIRST MEETING AGENDA (45 minutes) - 5 agenda items with time allocations - Questions to ask about their business goals - What to present to them 4. MONTH 1 COMMUNICATION SCHEDULE - Week by week: what you will send them and when Format each section clearly with headings. Use professional but friendly language.
Pro tips for best results
  • Run this once per new client — 30 seconds input, 2 hours saved
  • Customise the welcome email in your own voice before sending
  • Add the checklist directly to Karbon, Jetpack Workflow, or Asana
  • The meeting agenda works equally well for video or in-person
  • Combine with Prompt 1 after month 1 to build a full client reporting routine
  • Ask for a "difficult client version" to get more assertive language when needed
Example output (excerpt)
"Hi [Client Name], welcome to the team! We're thrilled to be supporting [Business Name] on its financial journey. Over the next week, I'll be setting up your Xero file — all I need from you to get started is your bank login details for the feed setup, last year's tax return, and a 20-minute call to walk through your chart of accounts…"
